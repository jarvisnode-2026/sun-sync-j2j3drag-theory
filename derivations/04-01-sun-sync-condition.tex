\section{Sun-Synchronous Constraint}

For sun-synchronous orbits, RAAN regression must match Earth's solar orbital rate:

$$\dot{\Omega}_{\text{required}} = \frac{2\pi}{365.25 \text{ days}} = 1.991 \times 10^{-7} \text{ rad/s} \approx 0.9856 \text{ deg/day}$$

\section{J2+J3 RAAN Rate}

From Phase 3:

$$\dot{\Omega} = -\frac{3 n J_2 R_E^2}{2 a^2}\cos i - \frac{3 n J_3 R_E^3}{2 a^3}\sin i(5\cos^2 i - 1)$$

\section{Sun-Sync Inclination}

For J2 only (dominant term):
$$i_{\text{ss,J2}} \approx \cos^{-1}\left(-\frac{2a^2\dot{\Omega}_{\text{ss}}}{3nJ_2 R_E^2}\right)$$

With J3 correction (first-order):
$$\boxed{i_{\text{ss}} = i_{\text{ss,J2}} + \Delta i_{J3}(a)}$$

where $\Delta i_{J3}$ is typically ~0.1-0.2° at LEO.

\section{Altitude Dependence}

At $h$ = 700 km ($a$ ≈ 7078 km):
- J2-only: $i_{\text{ss}}$ ≈ 98.2°
- With J3: $i_{\text{ss}}$ ≈ 98.1-98.3° (depending on detailed calculation)

\section{Key Result}

Sun-sync inclination depends on altitude. As $a$ decays (drag), required $i_{\text{ss}}$ changes.
