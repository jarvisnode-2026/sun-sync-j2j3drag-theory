\section{Objective}
Expand $P_3(\sin \phi)$ in orbital elements.

\section{P3 Expansion}

Using $\sin \phi = \sin i \sin(\omega + f)$ and $P_3(x) = (5x^3 - 3x)/2$:

\begin{align}
P_3(\sin \phi) &= \frac{1}{2}\left[5\sin^3 \phi - 3\sin \phi\right]\\
&= \frac{1}{2}\sin \phi\left[5\sin^2 \phi - 3\right]\\
&= \frac{1}{2}\sin i \sin(\omega+f)\left[5\sin^2 i \sin^2(\omega+f) - 3\right]
\end{align}

Using $\sin^2(\omega+f) = (1 - \cos 2(\omega+f))/2$:

\begin{align}
P_3(\sin \phi) = \frac{\sin i \sin(\omega+f)}{4}&\left[5\sin^2 i (1 - \cos 2(\omega+f)) - 6\right]\\
= \frac{\sin i \sin(\omega+f)}{4}&\left[(5\sin^2 i - 6) - 5\sin^2 i \cos 2(\omega+f)\right]
\end{align}

Using product-to-sum: $\sin(\omega+f)\cos 2(\omega+f) = [\sin(3(\omega+f)) - \sin(\omega+f)]/2$:

\begin{equation}
\boxed{P_3(\sin \phi) = \frac{\sin i}{8}\left[(5\sin^2 i - 6)\sin(\omega+f) - 5\sin^2 i \sin 3(\omega+f) + 5\sin^2 i \sin(\omega+f)\right]}
\end{equation}

Simplified:
\begin{equation}
P_3(\sin \phi) = \frac{\sin i}{8}\left[(10\sin^2 i - 6)\sin(\omega+f) - 5\sin^2 i \sin 3(\omega+f)\right]
\end{equation}

\section{Structure}
Multiple Fourier modes: $\sin(\omega+f)$, $\sin 3(\omega+f)$ — both oscillating terms for averaging.
