\section{Secular Nature of Drag}

Unlike gravitational perturbations, drag is **always secular** (dissipative, non-oscillating):

$$\langle da/dt \rangle_{\text{drag}} = da/dt \quad \text{(no oscillating terms to average out)}$$

For circular orbits, the drag rate Eq.~(2-03.7) is already the secular rate.

\section{Eccentricity Corrections}

For eccentric orbits, time-averaging $\langle \rho(E) \rangle$ over $M$ yields corrections $\propto e$.

\section{Combination with Gravity}

In Phase 3:
$$\frac{da}{dt}_{\text{total}} = \underbrace{\langle \partial R_{\text{grav}}/\partial M \rangle}_{\text{gravity}} + \underbrace{da/dt|_{\text{drag}}}_{\text{drag}}$$

Superposition holds (linear perturbations).
