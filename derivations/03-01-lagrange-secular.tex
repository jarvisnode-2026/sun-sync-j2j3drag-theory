\section{Time-Averaged Lagrange Equations}

For gravitational perturbations, secular rates are:

\begin{align}
\left\langle\frac{da}{dt}\right\rangle &= \frac{2}{na}\left\langle\frac{\partial R}{\partial M}\right\rangle\\
\left\langle\frac{d\Omega}{dt}\right\rangle &= \frac{1}{na^2\sqrt{1-e^2}\sin i}\left\langle\frac{\partial R}{\partial i}\right\rangle\\
\left\langle\frac{d\omega}{dt}\right\rangle &= \frac{\sqrt{1-e^2}}{na^2 e}\left\langle\frac{\partial R}{\partial e}\right\rangle - \frac{\cos i}{na^2\sqrt{1-e^2}\sin i}\left\langle\frac{\partial R}{\partial i}\right\rangle
\end{align}

where $\langle \cdot \rangle = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} (\cdot)\, dM$.

Only $k=0$ Fourier modes survive averaging.
