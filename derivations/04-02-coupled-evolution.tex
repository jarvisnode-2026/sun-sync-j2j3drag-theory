\section{Coupled System}

As altitude decays due to drag:
$$\frac{da}{dt} = -\rho_0 B\sqrt{\mu a}\,\exp\left(-\frac{a - R_E}{H}\right)$$

RAAN rate changes:
$$\dot{\Omega}(t) = f(a(t), i, J_2, J_3)$$

\section{Sun-Sync Violation}

If inclination is **fixed** but altitude drops:
$$\Delta\dot{\Omega} = \dot{\Omega}(a(t)) - \dot{\Omega}_{\text{ss}}$$

\textbf{Violation criterion:} When $|\Delta\dot{\Omega}| > \epsilon$ (e.g., 1\% of nominal rate).

\section{Characteristic Timescale}

For typical LEO ($h$ = 700 km, $B$ = 0.05 m²/kg):

- Altitude decay rate: $\sim$ 10-50 m/day
- RAAN rate sensitivity: $\partial\dot{\Omega}/\partial a \propto a^{-3}$
- **Timescale:** Months to years before 1\% violation

Exact value depends on $B$, altitude, and atmospheric density variations.

\section{Key Finding}

**J3+drag coupling creates secular sun-sync drift** not captured in J2-only or drag-only theories.
